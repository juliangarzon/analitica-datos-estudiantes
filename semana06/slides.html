What Makes a Strong Proposal?

Dataset Selection

  • From datos.gov.co
  • Sufficient rows (1000+)
  • Multiple variables (8+)
  • Mix of numeric & categorical
  • Recent and relevant

Problem Statement

  • Clear and specific
  • Real-world relevance
  • Answerable with data
  • Not too broad
  • Stakeholder identified

Research Questions

  • Exactly 3 questions
  • Specific and measurable
  • Connected to problem
  • Answerable with EDA
  • Progressive complexity
Remember: Your proposal is a contract with yourself. The clearer you are now, the easier your analysis will be later.

Research Questions: Good vs. Weak

Weak Questions

  • "What is the data about?"
  • "Is there any pattern?"
  • "What can we learn?"
  • "How does everything relate?"

Too vague - no clear direction

Strong Questions

  • "Which municipalities have the highest water consumption per capita?"
  • "Is there a correlation between population density and crime rates?"
  • "How has air quality changed in Bogota from 2018-2023?"

Specific, measurable, actionable

Pro Tip: If you can't immediately think of HOW to answer a question with your data, it needs to be more specific.

Peer Feedback Guidelines

Your feedback helps your classmates improve their projects

Good Feedback

  • "Your research question about X could be more specific by adding Y"
  • "I suggest visualizing the relationship between A and B"
  • "Consider whether Z might be a confounding variable"
  • "The problem statement was very clear and compelling"

Unhelpful Feedback

  • "It was good" (too vague)
  • "I don't like the topic" (not constructive)
  • "The slides were boring" (subjective)
  • "You should change everything" (overwhelming)

Feedback Framework: WWW

  • What worked well? (Be specific)
  • What could be improved? (Be constructive)
  • What question do you have? (Show engagement)

Handling Q&A

As a Presenter

  • Listen fully before responding
  • It's OK to say "We haven't considered that yet, but we will"
  • Be honest about limitations
  • Thank the questioner for their input
  • Keep answers brief (30-60 seconds)

As an Audience Member

  • Ask clarifying questions, not gotchas
  • Focus on methodology and feasibility
  • Be respectful of time limits
  • One question at a time
  • Build on others' questions
Remember: This is a learning environment. Questions should help, not embarrass.
